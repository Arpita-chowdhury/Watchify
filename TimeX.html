TIMEX

 Timex's roots date back to 1854 in Waterbury, Connecticut, a time when how you made something was just as important as what you made.
Timex Corporation is an American manufacturing company founded in 1854 as the Waterbury Clock Company in Waterbury, Connecticut. In 1944, the company became insolvent but was reformed into Timex Corporation.
In 2008, the company was acquired by Timex Group B.V. and was renamed Timex Group USA.
Thomas Olsen purchased the Waterbury Clock Company in 1941 and renamed it Timex, inspired by the names of Time magazine and Kleenex.
